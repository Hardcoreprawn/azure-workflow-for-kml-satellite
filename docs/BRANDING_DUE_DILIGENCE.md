### Key findings

- **treesight.com** is an active Mexican BI consulting firm offering data
  visualization, analytics strategy, and data warehousing. Their website
  displays "Treesight. Todos los derechos reservados 2025 ®" suggesting a
  registered trademark in Mexico. They operate as a consultancy, not a SaaS
  product, and are not in the geospatial/forestry sector.

- **treesight.co** is an active Israeli consulting firm specializing in smart
  cities, sustainability, homeland security, and crisis management. They work
  with governments and municipalities. While their industry differs from
  satellite forestry analysis, the "sustainability" and "environmental"
  keywords create moderate brand confusion risk.

- Neither entity operates in **satellite imagery**, **geospatial analysis**,
  **forestry monitoring**, or **EUDR compliance** — our specific niche.

## 5. Recommendation

### For demo/pre-launch (now)

**Continue using "TreeSight"** with these caveats:

- The name is safe for **demonstration purposes** and internal use.
- No commercial marketing or trademark filings should proceed under this name
  without legal counsel review.
- The Mexican entity's ® claim needs verification (jurisdiction, registration
  class, status).

### For commercial launch

**Consider renaming** before commercial launch, because:

1. Two active businesses already use the identical name.
2. The .com and .co domains are unavailable.
3. A subdomain-based production URL (treesight.hrdcrprwn.com) is not
   professional for a commercial SaaS product.

**Recommended shortlist for further evaluation:**

1. **Arbora** — Distinctive, Latin root, strong forestry connection
2. **Verdex** — Clean, modern, green association
3. **TerraSight** — Professional, earth + satellite imagery connotation

### Next steps

1. Verify Mexican trademark registration status and class coverage
2. Run domain availability checks on top 3 candidates
3. Conduct USPTO/EUIPO trademark search on shortlisted names
4. Engage legal counsel for formal clearance before any commercial use
5. Decide on final name before marketing materials are printed
