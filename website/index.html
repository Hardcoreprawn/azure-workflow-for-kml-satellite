Data Sources & Methodology

TreeSight uses publicly available satellite imagery and environmental data. Here's exactly where the data comes from and how we process it.

How We Process It

  • NDVI — Computed from Sentinel-2 10 m bands: (B08 − B04) / (B08 + B04). NAIP is used for visual context only, not for NDVI.
  • Cloud filtering — Scenes with >20% cloud cover are excluded. Pixel-level SCL masking is planned.
  • Weather — Queried at AOI centroid coordinates, aggregated from daily to monthly statistics.
  • Change detection — Same-season year-over-year raster differencing with ±0.1 NDVI significance threshold.

Known Limitations

  • Cloud filtering is scene-level only — some cloudy pixels may be included in otherwise clear scenes.
  • Weather is queried at a single centroid point, not a polygon-weighted spatial average. For large AOIs, this may not represent conditions across the full area.
  • Sentinel-2 revisits every ~5 days, but cloud-free images for a specific location may be weeks apart.
  • NAIP imagery is updated on a 2–3 year cycle and is not suitable for temporal change monitoring.
Questions

FAQ

What file formats are supported?
KML and KMZ files with Polygon or MultiPolygon geometries. You can create these free in Google Earth Pro — see our KML Guide.
How far back does the satellite imagery go?
Sentinel-2 coverage starts in 2018 for most regions. NAIP high-resolution imagery (US only) goes back further on a 2–3 year capture cycle. You get a seasonal timelapse for the entire archive.
How accurate is the NDVI change detection?
NDVI is computed from Sentinel-2 10 m bands — enough to detect individual field-level change. A ±0.1 significance threshold filters out natural seasonal variation. Weather correlation helps distinguish drought stress from land-use change.
Can I monitor the same area repeatedly?
Yes — that's what TreeSight is built for. Save your boundary to your library and re-analyse it any time. Each run captures the latest available imagery, so you can track change over months and years.
What happens if an imagery download fails?
Downloads are retried automatically with exponential backoff. If a source is temporarily unavailable, your analysis completes with whatever imagery was retrieved, and you can re-run later for the full dataset.
How large can my monitoring area be?
KML files up to 10 MiB are supported, with multiple polygons per file. For very large areas (e.g. entire concessions), break them into sub-areas for better resolution and faster processing.
Is the demo data real satellite imagery?
Yes — the demo shows live imagery from Microsoft Planetary Computer (NAIP at ~0.6 m and Sentinel-2 at 10 m). The same pipeline runs in production. What you see in the demo is what you get.
Can I export evidence for reports?
Analysis results can be exported as GeoJSON (for GIS tools) or CSV (for spreadsheets and databases). AI-generated findings are included. We're building PDF report export for future releases.
What happens to my data?
Uploaded KML files and analysis results are automatically deleted after 30 days on the free plan. Server logs are retained for 90 days. We never sell or share your data. You can export or permanently delete everything at any time. See our Privacy Policy.
